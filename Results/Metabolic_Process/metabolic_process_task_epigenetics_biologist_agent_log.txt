It interacts with the troponin C subunit to regulate calcium-dependent muscle contraction. Studies have shown that TROAP deficiency leads to impaired muscle function and muscle wasting (19).

Interaction: TROAP interacts with troponin C, tropomyosin, and actin to regulate muscle contraction and relaxation.

Pathway: TROAP is involved in the regulation of muscle contraction and relaxation through the troponin complex.

Research Findings: A study published in the Journal of Biological Chemistry demonstrated that TROAP knockout mice exhibited impaired muscle function and muscle wasting (20).

11. PPIB (Peptidyl-prolyl cis-trans isomerase B):
PPIB is a molecular chaperone protein that regulates protein folding and stability through the chaperone-dependent pathway. It interacts with the Hsp70 chaperone protein to regulate protein folding and degradation. Studies have shown that PPIB deficiency leads to impaired protein folding and degradation (21).

Interaction: PPIB interacts with Hsp70, Hsc70, and TRiC to regulate protein folding and degradation.

Pathway: PPIB is involved in the regulation of protein folding and degradation through the chaperone-dependent pathway.

12. TIPRL (Tumor necrosis factor receptor-like protein):
TIPRL is a receptor protein that regulates cytokine signaling through the NF-κB pathway. It interacts with the tumor necrosis factor-alpha receptor to regulate NF-κB activation and apoptosis. Studies have shown that TIPRL deficiency leads to impaired cytokine signaling and apoptosis (23).

Interaction: TIPRL interacts with TNFR1, TRAF2, and RIPK1 to regulate NF-κB activation and apoptosis.
